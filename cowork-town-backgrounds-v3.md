# Realm of Shadows — Town Background Images (v3)
## Painted Dark Fantasy Illustration Style

---

## THE CORRECT ART STYLE

The character sprites in this game (Paladin.png, Spellblade.png, Witch.png, Ranger.png, etc.) are **painted digital illustrations** — NOT pixel art. Analysis of the actual image files reveals:

- 1000-1300 unique colours per sprite (pixel art uses 8-32 colours)
- Average colour region width of 8-10 pixels — creating a slightly chunky, graphic feel
- Hard edges between major colour zones with NO soft airbrushing or glow effects between them
- Rich tonal variation within each material (armour has highlights, shadows, mid-tones — all as flat-ish painted areas)
- Black/dark outlines separating major elements

**The style is: dark fantasy digital illustration, painted with a bold graphic sensibility. Hard edges, confident brushwork, no soft gradients or glows. Think Darkest Dungeon, early Banner Saga, or Blasphemous concept art.**

---

## WHAT THE BACKGROUNDS MUST LOOK LIKE

Painted landscape/interior scenes in the same dark fantasy illustration style:

- **Atmosphere first** — these are mood images. A dim forest town at night. A forge lit by volcanic fire. A cathedral soaring into shadow. Prioritise feeling over accuracy.
- **Bold colour masses** — sky is one or two tone values, stone wall is 2-3 tones, shadow is deep. No photorealistic gradients.
- **Hard-edged forms** — building silhouettes read as clean shapes against the sky. Interior walls have clear stone/timber texture but rendered as flat painted areas with dark line-work.
- **Dark overall palette** — this is a dark fantasy game. Even "warm" interiors (inn with a fireplace) are predominantly shadowed. The fire is a bright accent in a mostly dark space.
- **Depth through value** — foreground darker than midground, sky lighter at horizon. Simple atmospheric depth without complex rendering.
- **No photo textures, no 3D renders, no watercolour washes, no soft airbrush** — painted, graphic, confident.

**Quality reference: Darkest Dungeon (the town scenes). Dark, illustrated, moody, confident paintwork. Detailed enough to feel like a real place but stylised and atmospheric.**

## CANVAS SIZES

- **Town exteriors:** 1440 × 900 pixels
- **Building interiors:** 864 × 900 pixels

For building interiors: the left 40% (x < 346px) will have a dark UI panel over it in-game. Put your most important detail in the **right 60%** (x > 346px). Both sides should be fully painted.

### `briarhollow.png`
Evening. A quiet stone-and-timber village at the forest edge. The main square has a stone well. The Wanderer's Rest inn is visible — timber frame, two storeys, a hanging lantern casting warm orange light. A smithy with a small chimney. Dense forest pressing in from behind, fog drifting between the trees at the treeline. Dark blue-grey sky with one or two stars. Warm amber lantern glow creates pools of light in an otherwise dim scene.

**Mood:** Humble, slightly melancholy, a starting point that already feels like something is wrong with the world.
**Palette:** Dark forest green, stone grey, warm amber lantern light, muddy earth brown, fog blue-white, night sky deep blue.

---

### `woodhaven.png`
Dusk. A village built into and around massive ancient trees — some buildings are platforms in the branches connected by rope bridges, others are built around the base of great trunks. A communal fire pit glows in the clearing below. Filtered light comes from glowing mushrooms and hanging lanterns made from hollowed wood. The canopy overhead is thick enough to almost block the sky.

**Mood:** Mysterious, organic, slightly otherworldly. Beautiful but not safe-feeling.
**Palette:** Deep forest green-black, bark brown, amber fire glow, bioluminescent green-gold mushroom light, deep shadow underneath.

---

### `ironhearth.png`
Night. A dwarven mining town squeezed into a narrow mountain valley. Low, heavily-built stone structures with iron-banded shutters. The Deep Forge dominates — a massive structure with a tall chimney belching orange-lit smoke against the dark mountain walls. Mining cart tracks glint in the lamplight. Iron bracket lanterns on every building. Everything has a thin coating of soot. The mountains press close on both sides, making the sky a narrow dark strip above.

**Mood:** Industrial, oppressive, loud, proud. These people chose to live inside a mountain.
**Palette:** Soot-black stone, iron grey, forge orange, coal black, lamp amber, sooty sky.

---

### `crystalspire.png`
Night. A city of slender arcane towers with crystalline observatories at their tops, glowing with cold blue-white light. The cobblestone streets have geometric ley line patterns inlaid that glow faintly blue. Robed scholar silhouettes visible in tower windows. Stars are unnaturally visible and numerous, even close to the city lights. The whole city has a blue-silver luminescence.

**Mood:** Intellectually beautiful, slightly unsettling, powerful. The kind of place that knows things it shouldn't.
**Palette:** Deep midnight blue-black sky, crystal blue glow, tower stone grey-blue, cobble grey, silver highlights, robed silhouettes.

---

### `thornhaven.png`
Late afternoon, overcast. A proper walled Imperial city. A wide cobblestone boulevard visible, deep red Imperial banners hanging between ornate lamp posts (gold sun symbol). Guard towers flank a grand gatehouse in the background. Noble townhouses with arched windows and carved stone. A fountain (dry, cracked). The wealth is real but the city has a tense, guarded quality — too many guards, too many locked doors.

**Mood:** Imperial power, wealth, hidden anxiety. The capital pretending everything is fine.
**Palette:** Imperial red-crimson banners, cobblestone grey-brown, banner gold, dark stone, overcast pale sky, lamp warm amber.

### `sanctum.png`
Twilight. The holy city viewed from the processional avenue approaching the Grand Cathedral. The Cathedral facade dominates the far end — massive white stone, soaring height, tall stained window arches glowing from within in red, gold, and blue. Thousands of candle-stands line the avenue creating warm pools of light. Pilgrim silhouettes moving in both directions. The sky behind the Cathedral glows unnaturally bright even as darkness falls elsewhere.

**Mood:** Awe, devotion, overwhelming scale. The architecture makes you feel small on purpose.
**Palette:** Cathedral white-cream stone, candle warm amber, stained glass red/gold/blue light, pilgrim silhouette dark, avenue stone grey, divine-lit sky.

### `saltmere.png`
Grey afternoon, drizzle. A rough port town on a dark choppy sea. Weathered wooden docks extending into grey-green water. Fishing boats and one battered merchant vessel tied up. Rope, nets, barrels piled everywhere. Rain-slicked cobbles. The Bilge tavern — a low building with a broken shutter — has a dim yellow light inside. Seagulls as dark shapes. The horizon is grey sea in every direction.

**Mood:** Rough, practical, nobody asks questions here. The sea is enormous and indifferent.
**Palette:** Grey sea, wet dark wood, rope tan, dark water, dim lamp yellow, rain-grey sky, salt-white foam.

### `greenwood.png`
Dusk. A wilderness outpost — more camp than town. A small clearing in deep forest with three or four sturdy wooden buildings: a ranger post, a supply cache with a reinforced door, a small shrine. A large campfire in the centre with a cooking pot. Forest walls close and dark on all sides. The sky through the clearing is orange-purple twilight, the only open sky visible.

**Mood:** Isolated, exposed, last safe stop before wilderness. The forest watches.
**Palette:** Deep forest dark green, firepit orange, timber brown, sky orange-purple, shadow black-green, earth dark brown.

### `emberveil.png`
Permanent sulphurous orange twilight — there is no real night here because lava provides constant light. Low basalt buildings built against a volcanic ridge. Lava channels run between the rocks, glowing intense orange-red. The sky is a haze of ash and volcanic glow, orange-grey. Steam vents create drifting white columns. Renn's Forge is the largest structure, its chimney adding more smoke to the already-thick air.

**Mood:** Hostile, visceral, dangerous but inhabited. People live here because they have nowhere else to go, or because they like being left alone.
**Palette:** Basalt black, lava orange-red, ash sky orange-grey, steam white, forge building dark brown, sulphur yellow-green.

### `the_anchorage.png`
Overcast dusk. A tiny fishing village on a windswept coastal promontory. Four or five weathered buildings facing into the wind. Fishing boats dragged above the high-tide line. A telescope on a tripod visible outside one building (Crystalspire researchers). Salt-bleached driftwood fences. The sea dominates — huge, dark, and close in every direction except the landward side. Wind-bent trees lean permanently away from the coast.

**Mood:** Small, exposed, at the edge of the known world. Ancient and quiet.
**Palette:** Sea dark blue-grey, driftwood grey-tan, building stone weathered grey, sky overcast pale, telescope brass glint, bent trees dark.

---

### `the_holdfast.png`
Ash-grey. Scorched earth as far as the eye can see. The Holdfast is a fortified ruin — walls patched with salvaged stone and timber, barricades of furniture and debris, makeshift fortifications. Inside the walls: campfires casting warm orange light on exhausted soldier silhouettes. In the far distance, the sky is wrong — a darkness beyond darkness where the Fading consumes the horizon, stars disappearing into it. The campfires inside are the only warmth in the image.

**Mood:** Last stand, desperate, beautiful in its defiance. The fires burn because people refuse to stop fighting.
**Palette:** Ash grey earth, void darkness (pure dark, no detail), campfire orange, crumbling stone, silhouette near-black, night sky dark with patches of void.

## INN INTERIORS (11 images)

**`briarhollow_inn.png`** — The Wanderer's Rest common room. Evening. Stone fireplace on the right wall, fire burning low and warm. Wooden tables with candles in bottles. A bar made from a single thick timber plank with barrels behind it. Deer antlers on the wall. Low beamed ceiling. Stairs visible in the back-left corner leading up. Warm amber glow, deep shadows in the corners.

**`woodhaven_inn.png`** — The Canopy Rest. An ancient tree trunk rises through the floor and disappears through the ceiling — the room is built around it. Furniture made from curved branch-wood. Hanging lanterns made from hollowed gourds, warm glow. A round window-door at the back. The wood of the walls is carved with forest shapes. Smells of pine and woodsmoke even from looking at it.

**`ironhearth_inn.png`** — The Anvil and Hearth. Built for dwarves — everything oversized. Stone benches and tables bolted to the floor, iron brackets. An enormous deep-set fireplace taking up most of one wall. Ale barrels in a rack ceiling to floor on the left. Iron chandelier hung very low. Rune-carved stone above the fireplace (decorative block patterns, not readable). Dark, warm, solid.

**`crystalspire_inn.png`** — The Ley Line Lodge. A scholar's inn. The fireplace burns blue-white (ley energy channelled into it). Crystal wall sconces glow faintly. Dark lacquered wood furniture, elegant. Bookshelves visible through an archway on the right. A small glass table with an arcane chess board. Feels more like a gentleman's club than a tavern.

**`thornhaven_inn.png`** — The Governor's Rest. Expensive. Red velvet curtains frame tall windows. A formal dining room visible through double doors with candles on white tablecloths. Framed portraits on the walls (faces turned away or obscured). A reception desk near the entrance. Everything is clean, formal, and slightly intimidating.

**`sanctum_inn.png`** — The Pilgrim's House. Stone cells visible through a long corridor on the right. A single long refectory table in the common room with simple wooden chairs. Candles everywhere in plain iron holders. A prayer alcove in one corner with an abstract sun/light symbol. Clean, quiet, austere hospitality.

**`saltmere_inn.png`** — The Saltwater Bunk. Hammocks stacked two-high in a low-ceilinged room. Rough plank floor stained with salt and worse. A single oil lamp on a hook. Rope and sailor gear on pegs along one wall. The "bar" is a plank over two barrels. A porthole window showing grey sea. There are no decorations.

**`greenwood_inn.png`** — The Ranger's Post bunkroom. Field maps pinned to every wall. Bedrolls on elevated wooden platforms. An open firepit in the centre — no chimney, just a hole in the roof above. Hunting trophies (skulls, antlers) on pegs. Trail gear hanging everywhere. Functional, rugged, lived-in by people who are often somewhere else.

**`emberveil_inn.png`** — The Ash Bunk. No wood (it would catch fire). Stone sleeping platforms with thin bedrolls. A small geothermal vent in one corner providing heat — and faint sulphur. The walls themselves are slightly warm to the touch; volcanic rock glows faintly orange in the cracks. Everything smells of smoke.

**`the_anchorage_inn.png`** — The Researcher's Bunk. Half the room is still a working research space: charts pinned to the walls, sample jars on shelves, tide tables. The sleeping area is behind a curtain partition. A small iron stove. Through a small porthole window: the dark sea, close. Cosy in a crowded, obsessive way.

**`the_holdfast_inn.png`** — The Commander's Quarters. A military bunkroom in a crumbling fort. Stone walls with cracks showing the Ashlands outside through them. Bedrolls on stone platforms. A map of the approach to Valdris' Spire pinned to the wall with markings on it. A single lantern. Through a partially collapsed section of wall: ash and void. People sleep here because there is nowhere safer within a day's march.

## STORE INTERIORS (8 images)

**`woodhaven_store.png`** — Mira's Forest Goods. Organic disorder. Shelves made from shaped branches. Baskets, root bundles, leather pouches, carved wooden tools. A cat sleeping on the counter. Daylight through the tree-window. Everything looks handmade or foraged.

**`ironhearth_store.png`** — Stonebacker's Goods. Dwarven precision. Heavy metal shelving, locked glass-front display cases for the expensive items. Quality goods visibly better-made than anywhere else. A mechanical counting device on the counter (gears and a stylus). Ingots visible on a back shelf. Dim, orderly, expensive.

**`crystalspire_store.png`** — Arcane Provisions. Very tidy. Glowing reagent jars in precise rows, each labelled. Focus crystals in lit display cases. A glowing catalogue orb on the counter (you tap it to search). No dust anywhere. The shop itself feels slightly magical — the jars cast small coloured lights.

**`thornhaven_store.png`** — Imperial Supply House. Large, well-organised, formal. Military-standard goods alongside civilian items. Imperial stamp seals on crate lids. A formal counter with a queue number system. Maps of the empire on one wall. Clerks in Imperial livery (silhouettes). Everything has a tax stamp.

**`saltmere_store.png`** — Finn's Dockside Goods. Deliberately disorganised. Salvage mixed with new goods. Mismatched shelves at odd heights. A suspicious pile in one corner with no labels. ## FORGE INTERIORS (7 images)

**`briarhollow_forge.png`** — Aldric's Smithy. One main anvil, coal forge with bellows, tools on a pegboard. Unfinished blades cooling in a water trough. Horseshoes and farming tools alongside adventurer gear — this forge serves everyone. Soot on every surface. The forge fire is warm orange. Small, capable, honest work.

**`woodhaven_forge.png`** — Oakbrand Smithy. Unusual — the forge is partially open to the forest, built against a natural standing stone that serves as the chimney. Specialises in nature-motif blades (leaf, vine, antler etchings visible on the display rack). A massive tree-stump workbench. The fire here has a faint green tinge from the wood burned.

**`ironhearth_forge.png`** — The Deep Forge. MASTERWORK. This should feel like an achievement of engineering. Multiple forge stations with complex bellows systems. A mechanical hammer arm (pulley and wheel, clearly dwarven craft). Racks of armour plates and weapon blanks in progress. The heat is intense — everything near the forges glows slightly. This is the finest forge in the game. Make it look IMPRESSIVE. Sparks, deep red and orange firelight, professional precision everywhere.

**`crystalspire_forge.png`** — Resonance Forge. The fire is blue-white, intensely bright, arcane energy focused by crystal resonators mounted on the walls. Very clinical. Weapon blanks glow faintly with arcane charge. The whole room has a cold luminescence except where the blue-white forge fire blazes. Precision work here, not brute force.

**`thornhaven_forge.png`** — Tanner's Armory. Imperial-contracted, busy. Multiple forge stations with worker silhouettes at some of them. Racks of standard Imperial-issue equipment alongside custom orders. Imperial insignia visible on some pieces. A quality-control bench near the front. Industrial scale, professional quality.

**`saltmere_forge.png`** — Harker's Rivet Shop. Ship-repair forge that also does blades. Rivets and ship fittings share space with daggers and short swords. Salvaged metal in bins. Everything has a coat of salt corrosion. Crude but effective. Smells of salt and oil.

**`emberveil_forge.png`** — Renn's Forge. MASTERWORK, volcanic. Built directly over a natural lava channel — the lava is visible through an iron grate in the floor, glowing intense orange-red. No bellows needed; the natural heat is sufficient. Blade rack holds Act 3-quality weapons. This is where the finest crafting in Act 3 happens. The lava grate should be the visual centrepiece — dangerous, beautiful, Renn's defining achievement.

## TEMPLE INTERIORS (6 images)

**`briarhollow_temple.png`** — Temple of the Flame. Small, worn, genuine. Stone walls with candles in every carved niche. A simple altar with a flame symbol in relief. Healing herbs drying on a wooden rack. An old painting above the altar (abstract flame shapes, aged). The floor is clean even though the rest is worn. A place of real faith.

**`woodhaven_temple.png`** — Grove Shrine. Almost entirely outdoors — or feels like it. A clearing between massive trees, a central carved standing stone with spiral patterns, offerings of flowers and fruit at its base. Filtered green light from the canopy. The "ceiling" is tree branches. The "walls" are living forest.

**`ironhearth_temple.png`** — Hall of the Stone Father. The entire back wall is carved in relief: a mountain shape, dwarven geometric patterns, the faces of ancestors barely visible in the stone. Stone benches in rows. Iron lanterns hung at knee-height casting dramatic upward light. The ceiling is low and vaulted with visible chisel marks — the stonework IS the worship.

**`crystalspire_temple.png`** — The Spire Shrine. Arcane-philosophical, not traditionally religious. Ley line diagrams glow on all four walls. A crystal sphere on the altar catches and refracts light into geometric patterns on the floor. Bookshelves with research materials. Students treating this as both shrine and study space. Sacred geometry is the religion here.

**`sanctum_temple.png`** — The Grand Cathedral interior. This should be the single most impressive image in the set. SOARING vaulted ceiling with ribbed stone arches disappearing upward into shadow. Impossibly tall stained glass windows (red, gold, blue coloured light falling in long beams across the stone floor). Rows and rows of pews receding into the distance. Thousands of candles on iron stands. The altar at the far end is gold-covered, enormous. AWESTRUCK is the only correct emotional response to this space. Take your time with this one.

**`greenwood_temple.png`** — A wayside shrine in a forest clearing. Just a carved stone post with a flame symbol, a few candles, and a small wooden figure left as an offering. Modest. One person's faith, not an institution's.

**`the_anchorage_tavern.png`** — The Salt and Scholar. Two cultures sharing one small space. Left side: fishing nets, maritime ropes, a ship's bell.
