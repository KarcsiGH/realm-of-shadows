# Realm of Shadows — Background Art
# SESSION B: Building Interiors — Inns, Stores, Forges (26 images)

---

## BEFORE YOU START — READ THIS CAREFULLY

This session generates exactly 26 images — 11 inn interiors, 8 store interiors, 7 forge interiors. Work through them **one at a time**, displaying a preview after each. Do not batch-generate. Do not skip previews.

### Style Reference

Open these two files before you begin:
- `~/Documents/RealmOfShadows/assets/sprites/characters/Paladin.png`
- `~/Documents/RealmOfShadows/assets/sprites/characters/Spellblade.png`

**Match the exact artistic style of these sprites.** Flat colour areas with hard dark outlines between major forms. 3-5 tones per material. No soft airbrush blending. Confident, graphic, dark fantasy painting. Like a detailed comic book panel or Darkest Dungeon concept art.

### Canvas Size
All building interiors: **864 × 900 pixels**

### Composition Rule
In-game, the **left 40%** (x < 346px) of each interior image is covered by a dark UI panel. Put your most important visual content in the **right 60%** (x > 346px). The left side should still be painted (it peeks around the panel edges) but the right side carries the scene.

### Quality Bar
These are the rooms players spend the most time looking at. Each one should feel like a real place — atmospheric, lived-in, specific to its town. Not generic. Ironhearth's forge should feel dwarven and industrial. Saltmere's store should feel like organised crime. Take your time.

## PART 1: INN INTERIORS (11 images)

All inns share these elements: some kind of sleeping arrangement visible, a bar or counter, a light source (fire, candles, lanterns), warmth. What differs is the scale, material, and character of each town.

---

### `briarhollow_inn.png` — The Wanderer's Rest

Stone fireplace on the right wall, fire burning low. A bar of thick timber planks with three ale barrels behind it. Two or three wooden tables with candles in bottles. Deer antlers on the stone wall. Low beamed ceiling. Worn wooden stairs visible at back-left leading to rooms. **Warm amber light, deep shadows in corners.** This is the archetypal fantasy inn — humble, honest, welcoming.

---

### `woodhaven_inn.png` — The Canopy Rest

An ancient tree trunk rises from the floor and disappears through the ceiling — the room is built around it. Furniture made from curved branch-wood, organic shapes. Hanging lanterns made from hollowed gourds, warm glow. A round moon-door at the back leads to sleeping areas. The walls are living wood, slightly curved. **Everything is organic, nothing is straight.** Green-amber light, very warm.

---

### `ironhearth_inn.png` — The Anvil and Hearth

Built for dwarves — everything oversized and built to last. Stone benches and tables bolted to the floor (no chairs — dwarves don't need them). An enormous fireplace taking up most of the right wall, fire roaring. Ale barrels in a rack from floor to ceiling on the left side. An iron chandelier hung very low, almost at head height. Rune-carved decorations above the fireplace (abstract block patterns). **Dark, warm, solid. Everything about this room says it will outlast you.**

---

### `crystalspire_inn.png` — The Ley Line Lodge

A scholar's inn, not a tavern. The fireplace burns blue-white (ley line energy channelled into it). Crystal wall sconces glow with cold blue light. Dark lacquered wood furniture, precise and elegant. Bookshelves visible through an archway on the right. A small glass-topped table with an arcane chess set on it. **Intellectual, refined, slightly cold even with the fire lit.**

---

### `thornhaven_inn.png` — The Governor's Rest

The most expensive inn. Red velvet curtains frame tall windows. A formal dining room visible through double doors on the right — white tablecloths, candles, proper chairs. Framed portraits on the walls (faces turned or in shadow — important people who don't want to be seen). A reception desk near the front. **Everything is clean, formal, and slightly intimidating. You feel like you're being judged.**

---

### `sanctum_inn.png` — The Pilgrim's House

Not quite a proper inn — religious accommodation. Stone cells visible through a long corridor on the right. A single long refectory table in the common room, simple wooden chairs. Candles everywhere in plain iron holders. A prayer alcove in the far corner with an abstract sun/light symbol. **Clean, quiet, austere. The hospitality of people who believe comfort is beside the point.**

---

### `saltmere_inn.png` — The Saltwater Bunk

Hammocks stacked two-high in a low-ceilinged room. Rough plank floor stained with salt, tar, and things best not identified. A single oil lamp on a hook in the centre. Rope and sailor gear on pegs along the left wall. The "bar" is literally a plank across two barrels. A porthole window (right side) showing grey churning sea. **There are no decorations. You sleep with one hand on your coin purse.**

---

### `greenwood_inn.png` — The Ranger's Post

Field maps pinned to every wall, some with routes marked in charcoal. Bedrolls on elevated wooden platforms. An open firepit in the centre of the room — no chimney, just a hole in the ceiling above it with smoke drifting up. Hunting trophies on pegs (skulls, antlers, claws). Trail gear hanging everywhere. **This room is a tool, not a comfort. People sleep here and leave before dawn.**

---

### `emberveil_inn.png` — The Ash Bunk

No wood anywhere (fire risk). Stone sleeping platforms with thin bedrolls. A small geothermal vent in the lower-right corner providing heat — steam and a faint orange glow from below. The walls themselves glow faintly orange in the cracks — volcanic rock still warm from below. The ceiling is low basalt. **It's tolerable if you don't think about what's beneath the floor.**

---

### `the_anchorage_inn.png` — The Researcher's Bunk

Half research space, half sleeping quarters. On the right: charts pinned to the walls, jars of coastal samples on a shelf, tide tables, a journal open on a desk. The sleeping area is behind a curtain partition (visible on the left). A small iron stove. A small porthole window (right side) showing dark sea very close. **Cosy in a cluttered, obsessive way — someone lives and breathes their work here.**

---

### `the_holdfast_inn.png` — The Commander's Quarters

A military bunkroom in a crumbling fort. Stone walls with visible cracks — through the largest crack on the right wall, the ashen Ashlands are visible outside. Bedrolls on stone platforms. A map of the approach to Valdris' Spire pinned to the back wall, with tactical markings on it. A single lantern hanging from a hook. **People sleep here because there is nowhere safer within a day's march. That is a terrifying thought.**

## PART 2: STORE INTERIORS (8 images)

All stores have: shelves of goods, a counter, lighting. What differs is what they sell, how they sell it, and who they're selling to.

---

### `briarhollow_store.png` — Harrow's Trading Post

Cluttered, warm, slightly chaotic general store. Wooden shelves packed with barrels, crates, coils of rope, bundles of dried herbs. A wooden counter with scales and a ledger. Pelts and dried goods hanging from the low rafters. Lanterns at different heights. Bare earth floor with sawdust. **The kind of store where everything is findable if you know where to look, which means only the owner does.**

---

### `woodhaven_store.png` — Mira's Forest Goods

Organic disorder. Shelves made from shaped branches — the store and the forest feel continuous. Baskets of foraged goods, root bundles, leather pouches, carved wooden tools. A cat sleeping on the counter (visible silhouette). Filtered green-amber light from a tree-window. **Everything looks handmade, foraged, or traded from someone who foraged it. No imported goods.**

---

### `ironhearth_store.png` — Stonebacker's Goods

Dwarven precision. Heavy metal shelving, perfectly level. Locked glass-front display cases for the expensive items. Goods visibly better-made than anywhere else — the quality is apparent even from the shelves. A mechanical counter (gears, an abacus arm). Raw ingots visible on a back shelf. **This store communicates one thing: inferior goods are not sold here.**

---

### `crystalspire_store.png` — Arcane Provisions

Meticulously tidy. Glowing reagent jars in precise rows, each casting a small coloured light on the shelf below it. Focus crystals in illuminated display cases. A glowing catalogue orb on the counter — the interface to the inventory system. No dust. No disorder. **The shop itself feels slightly magical — the jars form accidental constellations of coloured light.**

---

### `thornhaven_store.png` — Imperial Supply House

Large, formal, government-contracted. Military-grade supplies alongside civilian goods. Imperial stamp seals visible on crate lids. A formal counter with a numbered ticket system. Maps of the empire on the walls. Clerk silhouettes behind the counter in Imperial livery. **Everything has a tax stamp. Everything is logged. Everything is slightly more expensive than it needs to be.**

---

### `saltmere_store.png` — Finn's Dockside Goods

Deliberately disorganised. Salvage mixed with new goods on mismatched shelves at odd heights. A suspicious pile in one corner with absolutely no labels. A chipped clay bowl on the counter with coins in it and a hand-painted sign: "TAKE WHAT YOU NEED. PAY THE BOWL." The shopkeeper is never present. **The inventory changes daily. Some of it you don't want to know the provenance of.**

---

### `greenwood_store.png` — Trail Cache

A fortified storeroom. Metal-reinforced shelves with rations, rope, medicine, arrows, fire-starting kits. Everything has a clear purpose. A locked cabinet with a serious lock for the expensive or dangerous items. **Spare, organised, no-nonsense. Your life may depend on finding what you need in the dark. Act accordingly.**

---

### `emberveil_store.png` — Renn's Supplies

Compact, heat-adapted. Heat-resistant ceramic and iron containers for everything — no glass, no cloth, nothing flammable. Volcanic mineral reagents on one shelf glowing faintly orange. Fire-resistant materials folded neatly. Through an open doorway at the back-right: Renn's Forge, brilliantly orange and hot. **The shop is an afterthought to the forge. Renn sells supplies because people need them, not because she cares.**

## PART 3: FORGE INTERIORS (7 images)

All forges have: a main forge fire as the primary light source, anvils, tools, weapon or armour racks. The differences are enormous — from a village smithy to a masterwork dwarven industrial complex.

---

### `briarhollow_forge.png` — Aldric's Smithy

A village forge — one main anvil, a coal forge with bellows, tools hung neatly on a pegboard. Unfinished blades cooling in a water trough. Horseshoes and farming implements alongside adventurer gear. **Soot on every surface. Warm orange fire. Honest work for honest pay.** Small but capable. This forge serves everyone.

---

### `woodhaven_forge.png` — Oakbrand Smithy

Unusual: built partially open to the forest, against a natural standing stone that serves as the chimney. The standing stone is carved with spiral patterns. A massive tree-stump workbench. Display rack with nature-motif blades — leaf, vine, and antler etchings visible. The forge fire burns with a **faint green tinge** from the wood burned. Half smithy, half forest shrine.

---

### `ironhearth_forge.png` — The Deep Forge ⭐ PRIORITY IMAGE

**This is the most important image in this session. Spend extra time on it.**

A masterwork of dwarven engineering. Multiple forge stations each with their own bellows system. A mechanical hammer arm — a pulley-and-counterweight system, clearly engineered, distinctive dwarven craft. Racks of armour plates and weapon blanks in various stages of completion. The heat is INTENSE — near the forges, the metal glows. **Sparks drift. Deep red and orange firelight on everything. Professional precision in every detail. This is the finest forge in the game and it should look it.**

The mechanical hammer is the centrepiece — show it clearly. Multiple forges burning simultaneously creates a chaotic but purposeful light that illuminates everything.

---

### `crystalspire_forge.png` — Resonance Forge

The forge fire is **blue-white**, intensely bright — arcane energy, not coal. Crystal resonators on the walls focus the heat through magical means. The whole room has a cold luminescence except where the blue-white fire blazes. Weapon blanks glow faintly with arcane charge after passing through. **Clinical, precise, almost surgical. Power here is disciplined, not brute force.**

---

### `thornhaven_forge.png` — Tanner's Armory

Imperial-contracted, busy. Multiple forge stations, worker silhouettes at two of them. Organised racks of standard Imperial-issue equipment alongside custom orders. Imperial insignia on some pieces. A quality-control bench near the front with inspection tools. **Industrial scale, reliable quality. Everything gets made eventually, if you're on the list.**

---

### `saltmere_forge.png` — Harker's Rivet Shop

Ship-repair forge that also does blades. Rivets and ship fittings share the display with daggers and cutlasses. Salvaged metal in bins — some of it salt-corroded. Everything has a coat of oil and brine. **Crude but effective. Nobody in Saltmere is paying for pretty. They're paying for sharp.**

---

### `emberveil_forge.png` — Renn's Forge ⭐ PRIORITY IMAGE

**Second most important image in this session. Take extra care.**

Built directly over a natural lava channel. The lava is visible through an iron grate in the floor, the primary light source — intense orange-red, hot enough to be felt. No bellows needed; the volcanic heat is sufficient. The blade rack holds Act 3-quality weapons — the finest in the game. Renn's workbench is covered in high-tier materials.

**The lava grate is the centrepiece. Show it clearly in the right half of the image — the glowing channel beneath the iron grate, lava visible and threatening. Everything else orbits this.** This forge is genuinely dangerous and genuinely extraordinary.
