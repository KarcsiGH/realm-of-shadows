## PART 1: TEMPLE INTERIORS (6 images)

Temples range from an intimate wayside shrine to the most impressive interior in the game. Each reflects the religious sensibility of its town.

---

### `briarhollow_temple.png` — Temple of the Flame

Small, worn, genuine. Stone walls with candles in every carved niche — dozens of them at different heights. A simple altar with a flame symbol carved in relief. Healing herbs drying on a wooden rack above. An old triptych painting above the altar (abstract flame shapes, aged and faded). The floor is clean even though everything else is worn. **A place of real, quiet faith. Not grand. Genuine.**

---

### `woodhaven_temple.png` — Grove Shrine

Almost entirely outdoors — or feels like it. The "temple" is a clearing between massive trees viewed from inside a minimal roofed shelter. A central carved standing stone with spiral patterns. Offerings of flowers, fruit, and small carved figures at its base. Filtered green light from the canopy above. The "walls" are living forest. **The sacred space here is the forest itself. The shrine is just where you stand to speak to it.**

---

### `ironhearth_temple.png` — Hall of the Stone Father

The entire back wall is carved in relief: a mountain shape with dwarven geometric patterns and the barely-visible faces of ancestors worked into the stone. Stone benches in rows, heavy and permanent. Iron lanterns hung at knee-height, casting dramatic upward light on the carved wall. The ceiling is low, vaulted stone with visible chisel marks. **The stonework IS the worship. This hall was carved by hand over generations.**

---

### `crystalspire_temple.png` — The Spire Shrine

Arcane-philosophical, not traditionally religious. Ley line diagrams glow on all four walls — mathematical, geometric, beautiful in their precision. A crystal sphere on the altar catches the light and refracts it into geometric patterns on the stone floor. Bookshelves with research materials on both sides. Students using the space simultaneously as shrine and study. **Sacred geometry is the religion here. Understanding IS reverence.**

---

### `sanctum_temple.png` — The Grand Cathedral ⭐ PRIORITY IMAGE — MOST IMPORTANT IN SESSION

**This is the single most important image in this entire set of 55. Take as long as it needs.**

The interior of the Grand Cathedral. This must inspire AWE. Not appreciation — AWE.

- **Ceiling:** Stone ribbed vaulting arches rising to an impossible height, disappearing into shadow far above
- **Windows:** Tall stained glass windows on both sides casting long coloured beams of light across the stone floor — deep red, warm gold, vivid blue. These beams are the most important visual element.
- **Pews:** Rows and rows of wooden pews receding into the distance toward the altar — strong forced perspective
- **Altar:** At the far end, an enormous gold-covered altar with candles on tall stands flanking it
- **Candles:** Thousands of candles throughout the space on iron stands
- **Scale:** Everything should feel VAST. The pews make people look ant-sized. The ceiling is unreachably high.

The coloured light beams from the stained windows hitting the floor and pews is the centrepiece. When this image is right, a player will stop and look at it before clicking anything.

**Palette:** White-cream stone `#E8E0D0`, stained glass red `#C02030` / gold `#D09020` / blue `#2050C0`, candle warm `#D08020`, shadow `#1A1410`, altar gold `#C09020`, floor stone `#B0A898`

---

### `greenwood_temple.png` — A Wayside Shrine

Just a carved stone post with a flame symbol, a few candles, and a small wooden figure left as an offering. Set in a forest clearing. Viewed from very close. **One person's faith, not an institution's.** The forest around it is dark. The candles are the only light. Small, honest, and quietly moving.

## PART 2: TAVERN INTERIORS (8 images)

Every tavern has: a bar, some seating, a light source, and patrons (as silhouettes — never detailed faces). What differs is the atmosphere, the crowd, and what happens here.

---

### `briarhollow_tavern.png` — The Mossy Flagon

Warm, dim, lived-in. Ivy grows in the cracks of the stone walls — the tavern and the building are growing together. Wooden tables with candle stubs in bottles. A bar along the left wall with barrels behind it. Three patron silhouettes at tables in various states of relaxation. A fireplace at the back, fire low. A notice board by the door with papers pinned to it. **The definitive village inn. You want to sit here for hours.**

---

### `woodhaven_tavern.png` — The Hollow Stump

Built inside an enormous hollowed tree stump, expanded outward. The curved wooden walls have scenes from the forest life carved into them. The bar is a polished root section. Glowing mushrooms (blue-green) and candles provide the light — creating a warm amber and cool blue-green mixture. Low ceiling, the room curves. **Cosy in a way that only happens when you build a bar inside a tree.**

---

### `ironhearth_tavern.png` — The Iron Mug

Built for durability. Iron tables bolted to the floor, heavy stone stools. Enormous ale mugs visible. A challenge board on the right wall (drinking records, arm wrestling records — dwarven competitions). Barkeep silhouette large and solid behind the bar. In the left corner: two patron silhouettes clearly mid-argument (dramatic gestures). **Warm, loud, no-nonsense. The Dwarves here are proud of their endurance and want you to know it.**

---

### `crystalspire_tavern.png` — The Flickering Glass

Patron silhouettes at every table in animated discussion — gesturing, pointing at chalkboards, leaning across to make a point. Drinks glow faintly blue in crystal goblets. Chalkboards covering the right wall with equations and magical diagrams. Lighting is cold blue-white from crystal fixtures. A notice board of research commissions on the left. **Everyone here is the smartest person in any room they've ever been in. They'd like you to know that.**

---

### `thornhaven_tavern.png` — The Bronze Lantern

A city establishment. Distinctive bronze lanterns overhead casting warm amber. Better furniture than Briarhollow — proper chairs, better tables. Booth seating for private conversations along the right wall. A small raised stage in the far-right corner (a performer's stand, currently empty). Political pamphlet rack near the left side. Busy but orderly. **A place where deals are made in whispers and information is currency.**

---

### `saltmere_tavern.png` — The Bilge ⭐ PRIORITY IMAGE

**The most atmospheric tavern image. Make it memorable.**

Low ceiling hung with rope, old nets, and salvaged ship parts — a ship's wheel, a cracked lantern, a carved figurehead chunk. Every patron silhouette tells a story (one has an unusual hat, one has an arm in a sling, one is clearly watching the door). The bar is a ship's railing repurposed. Old nautical maps on the right wall, heavily water-stained. **A single oil lamp hanging from the ceiling, flickering.** A jar on the bar with no label containing something that glows faintly. The sticky floor is implied rather than shown. **This is the best tavern in the game for getting into trouble.**

---

### `sanctum_tavern.png` — The Quiet Cup

Near the Cathedral, so everyone here is either devout or pretending to be. Pilgrim and priest silhouettes at tables, whispering. Candles in religious holders (shaped like flames, small crosses, abstract light symbols). A painted eye-symbol above the bar — abstract, the Light's symbol, not threatening but watching. Clean, subdued, good wine in plain cups. **Nobody raises their voice here. Even the happy conversations are quiet.**

---

### `the_anchorage_tavern.png` — The Salt and Scholar

Two cultures sharing one small space, neither quite comfortable. Left side: fishing nets, maritime equipment, a ship's bell on the wall, weathered rope furniture. Right side: a chart table with maps pinned down, specimen jars on a shelf, a Crystalspire Academy pennant on the wall. A porthole window showing dark sea. **The fisherman and the researcher drink at opposite ends of the bar and occasionally, uncomfortably, talk.**

## PART 3: JOB BOARD INTERIORS (4 images)

Job boards are administrative spaces. They have: a physical notice board packed with papers, a counter or desk, and functional lighting. They're not glamorous — they're where work gets done.

---

### `briarhollow_jobboard.png` — Aldric's Notice Board

A covered notice board outside the guard post, viewed through the administrative room. The board itself is the visual centrepiece — packed with paper slips in various colours and sizes, overlapping, illegible but clearly a lot of them (job postings, bounty notices, lost animal reports). A counter with an open logbook. A guard post window behind, a guard silhouette visible through it. **Functional and slightly chaotic, like all village administration.**

---

### `woodhaven_jobboard.png` — Village Notice Post

The village administrative room. A carved wooden post visible through a window as the central notice board, notices tied to it with twine and hanging in the light. Inside: a village elder's desk covered in local maps, some hand-drawn. A wooden chair. A small carved animal figure on the desk (decorative). **Homemade administration. Surprisingly effective.**

---

### `crystalspire_jobboard.png` — Academy Commission Hall

A formal hall. A large slate board on the right wall with Academy commissions inscribed in glowing blue script (abstract patterns — illegible but clearly precise and organised). An administrative counter with a serious-looking functionary silhouette. Several student silhouettes examining the board. A waiting area with benches on the left. **Orderly, formal, slightly intimidating. The Academy takes its commissions seriously.**

---

### `thornhaven_jobboard.png` — Imperial Contracts Office

Government issue: dark wood panelling, numbered service windows along the right wall (like a bank), Imperial seal plaques above each window. Formal notice boards with stamped documents. Queue rope system in the foreground. Maps of the empire on the left wall. A clerk silhouette at the nearest window. **The most bureaucratic space in the game. You will wait in line. You will have the correct forms.**
